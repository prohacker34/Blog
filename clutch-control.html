Clutch Control and Gear Shifting

Diagram: Basic clutch pedal and gear positions.

5. Shifting to Higher Gears

  • When RPM reaches around 2500–3000 or when the engine sounds high, take your foot off the gas.
  • Press the clutch down fully.
  • Move the gear stick to the next gear (1st to 2nd, 2nd to 3rd, etc.).
  • Release the clutch smoothly while gently pressing the gas again.

Always press the clutch before shifting, and shift in a smooth, controlled manner.

6. Slowing Down or Stopping

  • Take your foot off the gas.
  • Press the brake with your right foot.
  • If your speed drops below 10 km/h, press the clutch fully to avoid stalling.
  • Shift to neutral and release the clutch and brake once fully stopped.

Common Nistakes with Clutch Control

  • Riding the clutch: Keeping your foot partially on the clutch while driving can wear it out quickly.
  • Releasing too fast: Letting go of the clutch pedal too quickly causes the car to jerk or stall.
  • Unnecessary clutch use: Only use the clutch when changing gears or starting/stopping.

Practice Tips

  • Find an empty parking lot or quiet road to practice clutch control without pressure.
  • Try moving in first gear at a slow speed using only the clutch — this helps develop a feel for the bite point.
  • Record how the engine sounds at various RPMs to get used to shifting by ear.
